Business Guide for Indie Game Company

Financing Strategies

Investment Environment

The external environment for the indie game industry is challenging; more than half of businesses fail to break even in the first five years, and many small studios have closed their doors due to a lack of competitiveness in China (Zhou, 2021). Although total capital invested in the industry has increased rapidly in recent years, the Matthew effect has become more pronounced: companies with a greater reputation are more easily financed and grow than those that receive little attention (Li, 2021). In light of these circumstances, I would advise novice game developers to apply for a game development job at a renowned game company's game department in order to build network resources, financial foundations, and insider knowledge before launching an indie game company. After three years working for a mature company, the game developer not only has the ability to start a gaming company from scratch, but also has access to capital at a lower cost due to the established personal network with potential gaming investors. Suggestions from previous indie game developers indicate that it is risky for a novice to invest the majority of his or her own money into a gaming company at an early stage, as most of the time the money does not pay off due to the industry's increased competition (Li, 2021). I'll compare various methods of financing a start-up indie game company in the following sections, but it should be noted that it is not recommended for a novice game developer without external financial support to start an indie game company.

Investors

At the beginning stage, angel investors and local banks are the most appropriate debtors. Because the capital requirement is not as large as it is during the growth stage, small cash inflows from bank loans with a lower cost of capital are preferred. Angel investors are less accessible but more appropriate because they can infuse the company with their knowledge, business network connections, opinions, and capital, and their required rate of return is typically reasonable due to their specialization in this field. For example, according to Qichacha (2021), Haoyue Network Technology Co., Ltd was financed by angel investors with a seed fund of approximately $5 million. It has successfully begun the third round of financing after three years after being provided with valuable growth suggestions.

The following are the reasons why alternative financing methods are not recommended:

1. Personal loans are not recommended because they are too risky for the company founder, and the bank's collateral requirements are typically prohibitively high for an average game developer without significant personal capital reserves. Additionally, the loan limit is low.

2. Crowdfunding is not recommended in China, particularly if the game's concept has not been censored yet. If the content or theme of the indie game is deemed inappropriate, crowd funding can easily be construed as illegal fundraising, resulting in legal consequences and a fine.

3. The government fund is inaccessible to the majority of indie game developers, as current policy favors pure tech firms over game development firms. There are indeed tax reduction policies for indie game companies as well as rewards for those with a high profit margin, but there are a few authoritative funds dedicated to support game companies from the start.

4. Venture capitalists are unlikely to fund an early-stage game company, as they are considered too risky for their portfolio.

Apart from bank loans and funding from angel investors, indie game companies that are short on cash should consider outsourcing some of their work to fund their own project. Another option is to develop a simple commercial game rather than a creative indie game in order to raise enough money to fund the development of the indie game.

Term Sheet Negotiations

Regarding term sheet negotiations during the investment process, I recommend that the indie company take a "rich" approach rather than a "king" approach, as the primary objective should be to successfully develop and deploy the game product, and a "king" approach ensures sufficient capital will be available throughout the process. Dividend and liquidation preference terms should be prioritized in this scenario. To ensure the game's smooth development and a high net profit retention rate, for example, the dividend payout ratio should be low, and investors should not have excessive liquidation preference rights. Nonetheless, board representation, antidilution clauses, and the right of first refusal may be compromised in part as a result, because venture capitalists are frequently more experienced in business operations, and having board representatives who are investors may result in a "win-win" situation for both parties, aligning the company's and investors' incentives. In an extreme case, investors could be considered "partners" in the business, which is why a partnership managerial structure works for a small percentage of existing indie game companies as well (while finding appropriate partners is harder than finding appropriate venture capitalists).

To address this issue, a dedicated CFO should be assigned the responsibility of negotiating with investors and providing accurate financial data in order to foster mutual understanding and trust. According to my prior experience working at a venture capital firm, investors in China typically require highly detailed industrial statistics, market potential estimations, how the company intends to fill a current gap, and the company's financial indices, particularly the profitability indices. To assist the CFO in securing external financing, the CEO should incorporate the CFO's recommendations when curating their business plans and developing their business strategies. The sooner an independent game developer incorporates financial targets into daily operations, the more likely it will be preferred by investors in the future.

Exit Strategies

The exit strategy for an indie game company, specifically in China, is tricky, because usually the company could be sold by 20x multiple current market value of the company to the potential buyers, even if the company is not profitable yet. How could it be possible for a potential investor to buy an unprofitable company?

Many media magnates are trying to gain more share of the market, e.g., Netease, Tencent, Tiktok, etc. They are competing very hard and all want to secure a greater market share at the early developing stage, so out of strategic reason, media companies compete to buy unprofitable companies they think may have a bright future and they offer very high price to those indie game developers. At all events, these investors are not short in cash (Ken, 2022).

With this information, we should pursue a strategic buyout of the indie game company rather than an initial public offering, as the frequency of gaming firms going public in China has been decreasing for three years (Wang, 2020). If we want to establish an independent game company in China, our first priority should be to avoid growing into a gaming company magnate unless we have substantial financial resources or well-known IP rights for game adaptation (e.g., the game adaptation of the Harry Potter IP indeed makes an indie game company successful in 2021). Instead, the priority will always be to differentiate ourselves from other players, whether through more advanced game design or more innovative game concepts, as these factors will eventually attract institutional investors and strategic investors. Only with the assistance of a strategic investment buyer can an independent game developer acquire the necessary capital and human resources to develop more sophisticated game products, because small indie game companies don't have the resources to compete with large media companies.
