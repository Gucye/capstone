Business Guide for Indie Game Company

Managerial Considerations

Capital Structure

The capital structure of the company should not be too aggressive during the first years. That says, the debt to equity ratio should not be high, or the potential effect of financial distress will be considerate and the regular debt repayment activities will significantly hinder the rapid growth and valuable investments of the company. Additionally, taking too much debt will strongly impact the decision making process of future potential private investors, because equity holders typically get paid later than the debt holders during a liquidation activity. To explain it more in detail, if the company takes too much debt at first and gets financed from an investor A, when the company unfortunately goes bankruptcy later, the banks will get paid by the residual value of the company first, and investor A will risk to get compensated for nothing. In conclusion, entrepreneurs should aim for a D/E ratio of 1 to 2 at first.

However, when the operations of the company normalizes, higher leverage can be built to take advantages of the tax shield. However, any debt-related decision should be made after evaluation of how the potential investors may think of it. Based on my prior experience in the venture capital industry, private equity companies will also prefer companies that can provide detailed financial data and measurements, so if there is a specific financial personnel in the company or if there is a management team member with solid basic financial knowledge, the company will be preferred during the due diligence process. In the rare case when the company decides to take on additional external funding by transferring into a partnership structure, the leverage may be lower to reduce the discount risks for partners.

Another aspect of the capital structure that must be planned for early is the ESOP (employee stock options plan). To incentivize game developers to work harder, companies nowadays typically plan ahead for how much equity should be set aside to reward employees, particularly the developers and art designers who are critical members of the team. In this case, 10% of shares could be reserved for the compensation program, and this should be communicated clearly to external investors during the negotiation process.

Organizational Structure

To begin, the company should establish a flat, decentralized organizational structure in order to reduce administrative time and facilitate collaboration between specialized personnel. Increased communication efficiency is critical for software development to ensure that design iterations run smoothly. One example of potential network effects is as follows: when marketing personnel provide the development team with information about a bug in the game's design, the development team promptly informs the product manager and frontend engineer via the flat network. Following that, an emergency meeting is convened with all pertinent personnel. With clearly defined tasks, a concurrent collaboration will occur, and the bug will be fixed.

Additionally, the system should include a hub node. The hub should ideally be an executive manager with a strong grasp of the business development strategies. He, along with the CFO who is familiar with projected financials, will be responsible for keeping the company on track. Apart from these functions, a divisional corporation structure may be advantageous during the company's later stages of development. A suggested divisional structure is as follows: product&marketing, game development coding, art&music design, and financial&administrative. The horizontal layout facilitates communication between divisions and effectively eliminates inter-departmental competition. In terms of finance, the divisional structure is more transparent and persuasive to venture capitalists during the second or third round of financing.

Managerial Structure

The managerial structure of current companies varies across the indie game industry, owing to the disparity in financial and operational support available at the start. If we are concentrating on a small game development team with limited resources, the sole proprietorship structure is recommended at the early stage. It assists in avoiding double taxation and the complicated corporate registration process, saving valuable time and cash flow for early-stage companies. After one or two years, the company should convert to a corporation for smoother financing rounds, as external investors or partners typically prefer a clear separation of equity in the term sheets. Additionally, investors typically require "collateral" or redemption clauses as backup guarantees for investments, which means that a corporation with standardized financial statements is the best option for securing potential investments by having everything organized. Finally, if the business intends to exit through a strategic buy-out, a corporation, as opposed to a partnership or sole proprietorship, will navigate the necessary legal processes more easily, as a corporation's information is more consolidated, if operated properly.
